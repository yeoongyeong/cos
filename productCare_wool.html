PRODUCT CARE

Wool

HOW TO WASH AND CARE FOR WOOL
  • Wool fibers have a natural protective barrier that prevents them from becoming contaminated with dust and dirt, so you don't have to wash them every time you wear them. Instead, hang them up to allow air to pass through.
  • Lint from friction is natural and can be easily removed with a lint comb or lint remover.
  • Alkaline detergent can affect the dyed wool and weaken the fibers, so hand wash with a mild detergent or machine wash on a mild course at 30 degrees or below.
  • It is better to spin at low speed or without spin. Squeeze out the water without twisting hard.
  • Dry it flat on a towel so that it does not lose its shape.
  • Wool knits are best stored after being air-dried and then folded flat.
ADVANTAGES OF WOOL

Warmth / Insulation / Thermostatic / Softness / Breathable

JWool is a fiber made from animal hair and is mainly used in sheep. The dense, soft threads create a natural barrier to block the wind and are somewhat waterproof. The warm and insulating properties of wool make it suitable for making knitwear. Small air pockets created by a pleated textile construction increase breathability and help regulate body temperature without being too cold or too hot. The zig-zag construction is stretchy and naturally elastic, so the garment recovers well to its original shape.
